SAT

The SAT is a paper-based standardized test developed by the american not-for-profit organization College Board, that tests skills like critical reading, writing, and mathematics, primarily for the purpose of undergraduate admission.

The test is scored on a scale of 200 - 800 points per section, totalling to a maximum score of 1600, and typically lasts for 3 hours without an essay section, or 3 hours and 50 minutes with an essay.

{% elif test_type == 'ACT' %}

ACT

The ACT is a standardized test used for college admissions in the United States. It is currently administered by ACT, a nonprofit organization of the same name. The ACT test covers four academic skill areas: English, mathematics, reading, and science reasoning. It also offers an optional direct writing test.

The test is scored on a scale of 1 - 36 points, and typically lasts for 2 hours and 55 minutes without a writing section, or 3 hours and 35 minutes with a writing section.

{% else %}

GRE

The Graduate Record Examinations is a standardized test that is an admissions requirement for many graduate schools in the United States and Canada. The GRE is owned and administered by Educational Testing Service. The GRE aims to measure verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, analytical writing, and critical thinking skills. The content of the GRE consists of certain specific algebra, geometry, arithmetic, and vocabulary sections.

The test is split into three sections: Analytical Writing, Verbal Reasoning, and Quantitative Reasoning. Writing is scored from 0 - 6 points, and both reasoning sections are scored from 130 - 170 points. The test lasts for about 3 hours and 45 minutes.
